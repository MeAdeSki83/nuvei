Nuvei’s Talents: Woodworking

Etsy Store: https://cornerofthegarage.etsy.com
Instagram: www.instagram.com/cornerofthegarage

Greg Wood is an instructional designer here at Nuvei. He is also an artist who creates stunning furniture and home décor pieces. We asked him to share his journey with us.

How did you get into woodworking?

I always wanted to do it, but I did not have a chance to start due to the lack of space. Once my wife and I moved into our new house that had a spacious garage, I finally got a chance to start building things I always wanted.

What was your first creation?

My wife always wanted barn doors, so I bought a saw and other tools and built them for her. I had to watch many YouTube videos to learn the basics. I later discovered some great Facebook groups on the subject, subscribed to some industry magazines, and bought a few books. There is a great community in woodworking! Very helpful and supportive.

What is your favorite piece?

Honestly, it is not easy to pick one because there are just too many I really like. But if I really had to, I would say it’s a coffee table. I found it at a small shop in Strawberry, Arizona. It was in a terrible condition – all dirty with rotten spots, so it took lots of cleaning and sanding for it to look decent. Once it was finished, I thought of selling it, but my wife insisted on keeping it.

How did you come up with the idea to sell your creations online?

The more things I created, the more people asked me to make something for them. In the beginning, I made bigger furniture pieces, but then I thought if I am to sell online, I need to focus on smaller size items that are cheaper and easier to ship. Custom-made furniture costs much more than any mass-market alternatives and many people are not ready to spend what it really costs. Smaller pieces were a good solution.

It was my colleague Lezlie Ho who helped me set up my Etsy store. She sells beautiful printable designs that people can download and print at home. Her store is called HeyMaeDesigns. Make sure to check it out: https://www.etsy.com/shop/heymaedesigns

How do your customers find you?

Social media and friends. For example, my colleague Jana Goldsmith bought one of my creations for her mom and because she is in Canada, it was my first international sale.

What do you like about woodworking the most?

It has been three years since I started my journey. I love that it is organic, practical, and artistic. It is a form of self-expression too. There are some very interesting trends happening in the woodworking movement right now. Artists are experimenting with inks and powders and different colorful patterns. It is very exciting!
